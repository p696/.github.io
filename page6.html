 Mt. Washington celebrates Independence day on July Third and     July Fourth.  We are a small Cincinnati neighborhood wih a population of less than 12,000 and simply can't compete with neighboring Anderson Township's 45,000 population in attracting celebration participation. For this reason, our main celebration takes place on the evening of July 3rd. The festivities are sponsored by area businesses, Mt. Washington Community Council, and Post 484.  Post 484 also is responsible for the the flags which line Sutton Avenue on some national holidays, such as Independence Day 
A parade forms at Stanbery Park, which was once the estate of General Stanford B Stanbery, one of the founders of Post 484. It proceeds through business and residential areas and finishes at Post 484.                                                                                                                   The post meeting hall is then opened to the public for refreshments, a guest speaker, entertainment and Cheese Coneys, courtesy of Gold Star Chili.  The Parade is led by Cincinnati Fire Department Engine Company #7, followed by Uncle Sam, our drill Squad, marchers, an old military vehicle or two, etc. After the parade, prizes are given to young children who particpated in a bike and trike deorating contest.
