About Post 484

 Legion and Post - Beginnings and Purposes

In 1919 a group of World War I officers met in Paris, France with the intenion of forming a veterans organization made up of those who served in that war. That organization was and is The American Legion. The reason for our being, as stated in the preamble to the legion's constitution, is "For God and Country." Several directives follow this. The first is "To uphold and defend the Constitution of the United States of America." Another urges us to provide service to "Community, State and Nation".

Last year the American Legion became 100 years old and next year Post 484 will celebrate their 100th anniversary. This makes us one of the oldest legion posts in the country. At Post 484 we strive to follow the directives of our founders. We do this through what we call our Legion Family. This family consists of Legion, Sons of the Legion, Women's Auxiliary, and Legion Riders, which is a motorcyle group, a part of Post 484 that accepts legion family members from both our post and other posts that do not have a riders group.

Together we support hospitalized veterans, donate to veterans substance abuse programs, award scholarships, participate in local events and have fun. Almost all veterans and decendants of veterans are eligible to join our legion family, so we ask that you browse our site by using THE ACTIVITIES AND SERVICES BUTTON at this and all pages, to learn if you wish to join us.

  Preamble to the American Legion Constitution

 FOR GOD AND COUNTRY WE ASSOCIATE OURSELVES TOGETHER FOR THE FOLLOWING PURPOSES:

To uphold and defend the Constitution of the                                   United States of America;
To maintain law and order;
To foster and perpetuate a one hundred percent Americanism;
To preserve the memories and incidents of our associations in all wars;
To inculcate a sense of individual obligation to the community, state and nation;
To combat the autocracy of both the classes and the masses;
To make right the master of might;
To promote peace and goodwill on earth;
To safeguard and transmit to posterity the principles of justice, freedom and democracy;
To consecrate and sanctify our comradeship by our devotion to mutual helpfulness. 

  What We Do

We believe that what gets done by the 484 family is more important than which organization does what. The Legion is involved in almost every event that takes place at the post and It is a strong source of workers and leaders for many events. Lists get to be boring and It is more practical to point out areas where all or most of the work is done by other branches of the legion family.
Some of these are: The Sons of the Legion (SAL) who are responsible for the Spaghetti Dinner, Lunch with Santa, the Cub Scout Pinewood Derby and Blue and Gold Banquet.
The Auxiliary Unit organizes and manages the Poppy Sales,has an annual Craft Boutique, and a St. Patricks Day Dance,                             The Legion Riders are a small group - only a few members who belong to Post 484.  Their primary duty is representing the post by participation in parades and other ceremonies that are in keeping with the aims and purposes of the American Legion.  They are also involved in many other legion family activities
